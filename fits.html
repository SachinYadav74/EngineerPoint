How to calculate Fits?

            In systems, machines, assemblies etc. parts are assembled. Depending on requirement their is gap between two consequtive parts. For example in gear box gears are connected to shafts with huge interference between gear and shaft. They cannot be dismantelled easily. In pen and cap there is transition gap but such assemblies can be easily dismantelled. Such mechanism is transition fit. In doors and hinges there is clerance gap. At such place no precision is required and it allow movements.

            Fits define the connection between assembled parts. There are three different types of fits .i.e clearance, transition and interference. Clearance fit means the movement between the consecutive surface will be free and with no friction. Next is transition fit defines that the movement will be press fit type. That is it will allow some friction. Next is interference fit, it says that there will be no movement between surfaces. It specifies that once the asssembly is done it will be very rigid. Fits are usually between holes and shaft. To calculate fits we must know the tolerance calculation. There are several tolerance classes. We denote them by capital letters (for holes) and small letters(for shafts). For example30H8/f7 The same nominal dimension is used for calculating hole and shaft tolerance.

Q1) Find type of fit for 30 H8/f7

Nominal dimension = 30

Maximum dimension = 30 + 0.033 = 30.033

Minimum dimension = 30 + 0 = 30

For Shaft

Nominal dimension = 30

Maximum dimension = 30 + (-0.020) = 29.980

Max clearance = Max dimension of hole – min dimension of shaft = 30.033 – 29.959 = 0.074

Min clearance = Min dimension of hole – max dimension of shaft = 30.000 – 29.980 = 0.02

Here maximum clearance and minimum clearance both are positive hence its a clearance fit.

  • If maximum clearance is positive and minimum clearance is negative then the fit is transition.
  • If maximum clearance and minimum clearance are negative then the fit is interference.
