What are different types of direction control valve/directional valve?

Oct 06

Direction Control Valve change the flow of cmpressed air. In Pure pneumatics circuit they are used for signallng elements(pushbutton, detent switches, lever operated) and for controlling actuators.

They are mainly classified on basis of

  • Numbers of ports available for connection
    •
  • Switching positions
    •
  • Home position
    •
  • Actuation method
    •

    Numbers of Ports

    Valves have several terminal for input, output and exhaust. These terminals are called ports. Direction control valves can be classified on basis numbers of terminals used. Some valves have 3 ports. Out of them 1 numbered port is always the input for compressed air. 2 numbered is putput and 3 numbered is exhaust. This is a standard followed in festo valves. Some have 5 ports. In such valves 1 is input for compressed air, (2,4) is for output, (3,5) is for exhaust. (10, 12) are used for changing positions.

    • 1 - Supply input
    • 2,4 - Output
    • 3,5 - exhaust
    • 12, 14 - postion change

    Home Position

    When valve is not activated from any side, its default position is known as it's home positions. This position can be normally closed or normally opened. Even connected to tank in case of hydraulics.

    Actuation Method

    The valve actuation method is a major classification criteria. Actuation with pushbutton as shown below, the valve actuates when pushbutton is pressed.

    It is spring loaded from another side, hence it returns to home position once released. Detent switch, lever operated, roller, one way trip lever are another types of valves actuated manually.

    Another method is pilot activated. The actuation which was mechanical is now pneumatically operated. When pneumatic air is supplied to their activating port the valve is operated. There are two ports for actuation while some have one. Valves with one pilot port are spring loaded to return to home position. Valves with two pilot ports are also known as memory valve as they remain in actuated port even after pilot port supply is off. When opposite pilot port recives signal then position is changed.
