How to calculate Tolerance?

             Tolerance itself means scope of deviation in dimension. It sepecifies what can be the maximun and minimum deviation for a given dimension. Tolerance has a upper deviation limit and lower deviation limit. With that we can specify a upper dimension i.e what can be the maximum dimension and lower dimension i.e. what can be the minimum dimension. For example. 25Â±0.2mm.

Nominal Dimension = 25

Upper deviation = +0.2

Lower deviation = -0.2

Upper Dimension = Nominal dimension + Upper deviation

Upper Dimension = 25 + (+0.2) = 25.2........(1)

Lower Dimension = Nominal dimension + Lower deviation

Lower Dimension = 25 + (-0.2) = 24.8........(2)

Tolerance = Upper dimension - Lower Dimension

Tolerance = 25.2 - 24.8 = 0.4........(3)

            Here we have upper deviation of 0.2mm and lower deviation of 0.2mm. So we can now calculate 25+0.2=25.2mm is the upperlimit of the dimesion gievn. Now 25-0.2=24.8mm is the lower limit. Now we can calculate Tolerance. It is Upper limit - Lower limit. i.e. 25.2 - 24.8=0.4mm is the tolerance. There are standard tables specified by ISO standard for tolerance classes.
