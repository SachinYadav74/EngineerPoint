Ohm's Law

What is ohm's law? | Calculator

Nov 07

Ohm's law states that current through a conductor between two point is directly proportional to Voltage across two point.

            In a closed circuit when potential difference is applied, current flows. the Current flow from positive terminal to negative terminal. The intensity of current depends on the resistance connected to it's path. the Resistance is any obstacle like bulb, motor, sound or even a resistor. All these's have different power rating and current requirement. Similarly they also have their own Fixed resistance.

            Ohm's law gives a equation with that we can calculate current, voltage & Resistance. Equation is V = I * R

  •             V is voltage
  •             I is Current
  •             R is resistance

            In a closed circuit, if Voltage is constant Resistance is variable for then current valve will depend on resistance. Higher is the resistance lower is the current. Current is inversely proportional to resistance.

             In a closed circuit if resistance is constant voltage is increasing current value will also increase. Current is directly proportional to voltage.
