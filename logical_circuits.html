Logical Circuit | Simple, Dependent, Relay,Interlock, Latching

Nov 07

            In automation circuit output Signal are depended on different input & interlocks . For example there is a conveyor having door at its end. The conveyor should not start as long as the door is closed. When door is open the conveyor receives the Signal for actuation then only conveyors should start. Such circuit is dependent. The conveyor is depended door input signal.

            Lets take another example, suppose there is a motor which rotates clockwise and anticlockwise. But if motor is rolating clockwise and when signal for anticlockwise is received, the motor should not start rotating anticlockwise. Motor should keep rotating clockwise. And if motor is rotating anticlockwise it should not rotate clockwise even if button for clockwise is pressed. Such circuit is called interlock circuit. Lets make such circuit.

Simple Circuit

            Simple circuits are very easy to understand. There is a 24V supply with a pushbutton and lamp connected. Initialy the circuit is open. When pushbutton is pressed then circuit is closed and lamp lights up. Video

Relay Circuit

            In relay circuit a pushbutton is connected to relay and auxilary contacts are used to connect bulb or any other load. Here when pushbutton (S1 or S2) is pressed. Relay (K1 or K2) is activated Parallely the relays contact is used. The Normally open contact closes and lamp lights up. Video

Dependent Circuit

            In dependent circuit when first condition is achived only then the another step is activated. For example, Here when lamp lights up only then motor will start. One No contact is connected in series to the circuit of relay that will be activating motor. Video

Interlock Circuit

            In interlock circuit, if one step is active another step does not get executed, One Normally Closed contact of initial step is connected in series. Due to this when step one is active it's relay's NC contact opens, and disconnects the supply to relay of another circuit. Video

Latching Circuit

            Suppose you have a normally open pushbutton, relay and a lamp Now you want that when you preess button lamp should glow and when you release button the lamp should still remain on. In that case latching is used. for example in below circuit if button S1 is pressed relay gets activated and when S2 is pressed lamp gets off.

            A parallel NO contact of relay is connected to relay's A1 port with a NC button between to switch off the circuit. when pushbutton is pressed supply go from S1 to S2 to A1 of relay. From K1(3,4) supply goes to k1's A1 port. Hence, the relay remains latched. By pressing S2, Supply is turned off and relay is deactivated. Video
